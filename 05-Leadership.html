5  Leadership and Headquarters Bandwidth

5.1 Percentage of Leaders 3+ years in role

These metrics measure how long individuals in specific leadership positions have been in their current role at the time the data is pulled. For example, if the current executive director was held an executive director position for 3 or more years over the course of their career with IDEA (2 years as Executive Director & 1 year as Senior Executive Director), they have been in their role for 3+ years. However, if someone changed between roles, such as spending 2 years as an Assistant Principal of Instruction (API) followed by 1 year as a Principal, they have not been in a role of Principal for 3 or more years.

In the Fall 2025 scorecards, these metrics represent the leaders who were in their position at the end of the 2024-2025 school year (May 2025) rather than the start of the the 2025-2026 school year.

5.1.1 Percentage of Executive Directors 3+ Years in Role

Executive Directors include positions with variations of the following job title:

  • Executive Director
  • Senior Executive Director

5.1.2 Percentage of Principals 3+ Years in Role

Principals include positions with variations of the following job title:

  • Principal
  • Executive Principal
  • Senior Executive Principal
  • Principal K-8

We previously have not included the following job titles as principals:

  • Rhodes Principal Fellow
  • Successor Principal Fellow
  • Principal in Residence

5.1.3 Percentage of APIs 3+ Years in Role

Assistant Principal of Instruction (API) positions include positions with variations of the following job title:

  • Assistant Principal of Instruction
  • Senior Principal of Instruction
  • Assistant Principal of Instruction-Intervention

We previously have not included the following job titles as principals:

  • Assistant Principal of Instruction in Residence

5.1.4 Notes & Exclusions

  • As noted above, we have not included “fellow” or “in residence” positions API, Principal, or Executive Director positions.

5.2 API Vacancy Rate (Expected + New)

This metric is intended to measure the percent of Assistant Principal of Instruction (API) positions are vacant at a certain snapshot date (e.g. July, or start of school). We will calculate this metric as:

\[ \text{ API Vacancy Rate} = \left(1 - \frac{\# \text{ of Filled API Positions}}{\# \text{ of Active API Positions}}\right)*100 \]

Where Filled Positions refer to positions in which there is currently an active employee working at the time of the snapshot and Active Positions refer to API positions which are either currently filled or hiring.

5.2.1 Notes & Exclusions

  • We did not have access to the relevant data in time to include it in the Fall 2025 scorecards.
  • We will exclude “pending” API positions, which indicate a position is planned for next year.
  • At any given time, positions that have been hired but in which the employee has not been processed in Tyler Munis will show up as vacant.
  • As with all data, data are only accurate for the specific time frame indicated. For example, if a principal steps down from their role after the data have been pulled, that principals position will show as filled.

They should be able to provide a selection of data from their “Master Roster” report for the months of interest. These reports are run on the first workday of each month. 5.3 Number of CAP Graduates (2-Year)

IDEA launched the Cadre of Aspiring Principals (CAP) program during the 2022-2023 school year, graduating their first cohort in spring 2024.

The Cadre of Aspiring Principals (CAP) program empowers regions to build a strong bench of aspiring principals so that every school has a transformational leader year after year. 

The CAP program builds the ability of aspiring principals to lead systems and lead leaders at the current school. Since participants choose one track (Leading Systems or Leading Leaders) each year, leaders are encouraged to participate for 2 years if possible. After completing CAP, leaders are prepared to apply their learnings in Successor Principal Academy (SPA) if they will become successor principals or the Rhodes Fellowship if they will become launching principals.  (IDEA Public Schools Human Assets 2025)

Fall 2025 scorecard data include the total count of all historical CAP graduates.

5.3.1 Notes & Exclusions

  • Rachel Willcutts, Managing Director of Leader Development, shared some important context surrounding the CAP Graduates data:(Willcutts 2025)

    • Many more leaders enroll in the CAP program than graduate from it, for positive reasons. Many who enroll do not complete the full two years, because they are promoted to the Successor Principal Academy or even hired to principal roles prior to finishing.

    • However, “the fact that most are promoted before finishing 2 years suggests that the current demand for aspiring principals to fill vacancies at existing schools meets or exceeds the number of aspiring principals in most of our regions. (The RGV is the one clear exception; in that region, the number of aspiring principals exceeds the number of vacancies.)”

5.3.2 Source

Records for number of individuals completing 2 years of CAP were provided at the regional level by Rachel Willcutts. She will continue to be our source for this data in future iterations of the scorecard.
